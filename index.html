Projects

CanSat — Remote-Controlled Parachute

Embedded Telemetry C/C++ Materials Aero & data

I developed a CanSat payload with a remote-controlled parachute focusing on stable descent, reliable telemetry, and rugged design. The project combined lightweight materials selection, canopy geometry, and control logic to steer and manage descent rate while transmitting live data.

Embedded & Telemetry

  • Firmware in C/C++ (sensor fusion, state machine for deployment & steering).
  • Live downlink (altitude, velocity, temperature) with packet integrity checks.
  • Ground control for manual inputs and basic autonomous safety routines.

Materials & Structure

  • Canopy fabric selection (tear resistance, mass, stitch patterns) and line routing.
  • Payload frame designed for impact tolerance & easy access to electronics.
  • Environmental sealing and strain relief on connectors.

Outcomes

  • Stable, steerable descent with predictable glide ratio.
  • Consistent telemetry logs for flight analysis and refinement.
  • Lessons transferable to eco-design and reliable prototyping.

Result: a robust, testable platform that taught me the same rigor as larger IoT or mechatronics projects—only in a compact, high-stakes format.
